# Ton News moderation policy

This policy gives moderators a repeatable process for reports, appeals, and
content decisions.

## Moderation principles

- Act on evidence, not assumptions.
- Prefer correction over removal for good-faith mistakes.
- Move quickly on safety issues such as phishing, private data, malware, or
  harassment.
- Keep a short audit trail for every rejected, removed, or escalated item.
- Avoid conflicts of interest. A moderator should not be the final reviewer of
  their own content.

## Report intake

Reports should include:

- URL or submission title
- Reporter contact, if follow-up is needed
- Reason for the report
- Source links, screenshots, or other supporting context
- Whether urgent user safety is involved

If the report alleges credential theft, private key exposure, phishing, or
personal safety risk, treat it as urgent and hide the content while reviewing.

## Triage levels

| Level | Examples | Target response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Critical | Phishing, malware, private keys, doxxing, credible threats | Hide immediately, review within 4 hours |
| High | Harassment, impersonation, undisclosed paid promotion, major factual errors | Review within 24 hours |
| Normal | Duplicate content, weak sourcing, style or relevance issues | Review within 3 business days |
| Low | Typos, formatting, tag corrections | Batch with regular editorial work |

## Review procedure

1. Record the report and triage level.
2. Preserve the reported content or screenshot for context.
3. Compare the content against the community guidelines.
4. Ask the author for clarification when the issue is not urgent.
5. Decide: no action, edit request, reject, remove, restrict, or escalate.
6. Document the decision and notify the reporter when appropriate.

## Appeals

Authors can appeal rejections, removals, or restrictions by providing:

- The original submission or content URL
- The moderation decision being appealed
- A concise explanation of why the decision should change
- Any corrected sources or revised text

Appeals should be reviewed by a moderator who did not make the original
decision. Outcomes are: uphold, revise, or reverse the decision.

# Ton News community guidelines

Ton News is a community-edited publication for people learning, building, and
reporting on The Open Network (TON). These guidelines define what belongs on the
platform and how contributors should work together.

## Expected behavior

- Be accurate, constructive, and respectful.
- Share source links for factual claims.
- Disclose conflicts of interest, sponsorships, or token incentives.
- Critique ideas, projects, and code without personal attacks.
- Respect privacy. Do not publish private contact details, private keys,
  unreleased credentials, or non-public personal information.
- Keep submissions relevant to TON, its ecosystem, or adjacent developer and
  user education.

## Acceptable content

- TON protocol updates and explainers
- Wallet, validator, staking, and developer guides
- Ecosystem project analysis and interviews
- Security, reliability, and risk education
- Community event, grant, and hackathon coverage
- Opinion or analysis when clearly labeled and source-backed

## Unacceptable content

- Harassment, hate, threats, or personal attacks
- Plagiarism or unattributed AI-generated bulk content
- Investment advice, price predictions, or guaranteed-return claims
- Undisclosed paid promotion or affiliate marketing
- Malware, phishing, credential harvesting, or unsafe wallet instructions
- Doxxing or publication of private personal information
- Spam, duplicate submissions, or off-topic promotional posts

## Enforcement actions

Moderators may take one or more actions depending on severity:

- Request edits before publication
- Reject a submission with a reason
- Remove or unpublish content
- Temporarily limit submission access
- Permanently block abusive contributors
- Escalate legal, safety, or security issues to project maintainers

Moderation should be proportional, documented, and reversible when a contributor
fixes the underlying issue.

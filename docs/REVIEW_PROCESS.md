# Ton News review process

This process keeps community submissions consistent, accurate, and ready for
search indexing.

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Contributor | Drafts the article and includes source links. |
| Editor | Reviews topic fit, structure, style, and SEO basics. |
| Fact checker | Verifies factual claims against primary sources. |
| Moderator | Approves, rejects, or requests revisions in the moderation queue. |

One person can hold multiple roles on small teams, but the final approval should
not be done by the original author when possible.

## Workflow

1. Contributor submits an article through `/submit`.
2. Editor checks whether the topic fits the calendar and avoids duplication.
3. Fact checker verifies core claims, numbers, names, and dates.
4. Editor applies the SEO and style checklist.
5. Moderator approves the article or rejects it with a clear note.
6. Published article URL is added back to the content calendar.

## Review checklist

- Topic fits one of the editorial pillars.
- Title and excerpt match the article body.
- Claims are backed by source links.
- No price prediction, investment advice, or undisclosed promotion.
- Markdown is readable and organized with headings where useful.
- Slug, tags, and excerpt are search-friendly.
- Related internal links are added when available.

## Decision guide

| Decision | Use when |
| --- | --- |
| Approve | Article is accurate, clear, sourced, and ready to publish. |
| Request changes | Article is promising but needs sources, structure, or style edits. |
| Reject | Article is off-topic, promotional, unsafe, duplicate, or unsourced. |

## Reviewer note examples

```txt
Approved: source links verified, title and excerpt are search-friendly.
```

```txt
Request changes: please add primary sources for the validator statistics and
clarify the publication date of the referenced upgrade.
```

```txt
Rejected: promotional copy without sufficient editorial value or source links.
```

# Ton News editorial guidelines

Ton News publishes practical, source-backed coverage of The Open Network (TON)
for builders, users, and ecosystem observers.

## Editorial standards

- Lead with the useful answer. Each article should explain what changed, why it
  matters, and what readers can do next.
- Cite primary sources when possible: official project posts, documentation,
  repositories, governance threads, dashboards, or on-chain data.
- Separate fact, interpretation, and opinion. Label analysis clearly.
- Avoid investment advice, price predictions, and promotional claims.
- Disclose sponsorships, affiliations, or token incentives.
- Prefer clear language over jargon. Define TON-specific terms on first use.

## Style guide

- Use `TON` for the ecosystem and token unless a source uses a formal product
  name.
- Use `The Open Network` on first mention, then `TON`.
- Write headlines that include the primary search phrase and a concrete benefit.
- Keep paragraphs short enough for mobile reading.
- Use descriptive link text instead of "click here".
- Include dates for time-sensitive claims.

## Article structure

Recommended structure for most posts:

1. Summary paragraph with the key takeaway.
2. Context: what this topic is and who it affects.
3. Details: source-backed explanation, data, or walkthrough.
4. Practical implications for users, developers, or validators.
5. Related links and next steps.

## SEO checklist

- One clear H1.
- Short excerpt of 120-180 characters.
- Primary keyword in title, excerpt, and first paragraph when natural.
- At least one internal link to a related Ton News article when available.
- External links point to authoritative sources.
- Slug is lowercase, descriptive, and hyphenated.
- Tags are specific and reusable, such as `validators`, `wallets`, `defi`, or
  `developer-tools`.

## Submission requirements

Submissions should include:

- Title
- Excerpt
- Body in Markdown
- Tags
- Author name
- Source links for factual claims

Editors may reject or request changes for unsourced claims, duplicate topics,
thin summaries, promotional copy, unsafe financial advice, or unclear authorship.
